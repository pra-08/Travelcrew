Day 1:Delhi/Chandigarh to Ujjain (Mahakaleshwar)

Commence your spiritual journey from Delhi/Chandigarh towards the sacred city of Ujjain, home to the revered Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga. Board the Volvo from the designated pick-up point and enjoy a comfortable overnight journey.

Day 2:Ujjain(Mahakaleshwar) Darshan

Arrive in Ujjain in the morning and check-in at the hotel. After freshening up, proceed to the Mahakaleshwar Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas dedicated to Lord Shiva. Seek blessings at the temple and witness the mesmerizing Bhasma Aarti, a sacred ritual performed with ashes. Explore other significant temples in Ujjain such as Kal Bhairav Temple, Harsiddhi Temple, and Chintaman Ganesh Temple. Return to the hotel for dinner and overnight stay in Ujjain.

Day 3:Ujjain to Omkareshwar

After breakfast, embark on a scenic drive to Omkareshwar, another sacred pilgrimage destination known for the Omkareshwar Jyotirlinga temple. Upon arrival, check-in at the hotel and freshen up. Visit the Omkareshwar Temple, located on the banks of the Narmada River, and seek blessings from Lord Shiva. Explore the serene surroundings of Omkareshwar and take a peaceful stroll along the ghats. Dinner and overnight stay at the hotel in Omkareshwar.

Day 4:Omkareshwar Exploration

Start your day with a visit to the Siddhanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva's Siddhanath incarnation, and the nearby Kedareshwar Temple. Enjoy a boat ride on the Narmada River, offering panoramic views of the temple complex and surrounding landscapes. Explore the quaint streets of Omkareshwar and indulge in local delicacies. Spend the evening attending the evening aarti ceremony at the Omkareshwar Temple, filled with devotional chants and rituals. Dinner and overnight stay at the hotel.

Day 5:Departure from Omkareshwar

After breakfast, bid farewell to the divine land of Omkareshwar as you head back to Delhi/Chandigarh via Volvo. Depart with divine blessings and cherished memories of your spiritual journey through the sacred cities of Ujjain (Mahakaleshwar) and Omkareshwar.
